Unit 1 — Content & Understanding

Parametrics let us describe motion/paths via \(x(t),y(t)\). In polar, \(r(\theta)\) makes area/length compact for radially symmetric curves. Conics unify in polar using eccentricity \(e\).

Problem 1 — Parametric: area and arc length of a cycloid arch

Given A rolling circle of radius \(r\) with a marked rim point generates \(x=r(t-\sin t)\), \(y=r(1-\cos t)\), \(0\le t\le2\pi\).

Find (a) the area under one arch, (b) the length of that arch.

  1. Area formula (parametric). \[ A=\int y\,dx=\int_{0}^{2\pi}y(t)\,x'(t)\,dt \] Here \(y=r(1-\cos t)\), \(x'=r(1-\cos t)\).
  2. Compute \(A\). \[ A=r^2\!\int_{0}^{2\pi}(1-\cos t)^2\,dt = \boxed{3\pi r^2} \]
  3. Arc length (parametric). \[ L=\int_{0}^{2\pi}\!\sqrt{(x')^2+(y')^2}\,dt,\quad x'=r(1-\cos t),\ y'=r\sin t. \]
  4. Compute \(L\). \[ L=r\!\int_{0}^{2\pi}2\Big|\sin\frac t2\Big|\,dt = \boxed{8r}. \]

Formulas used: \(A=\int g(t)f'(t)\,dt\), \(L=\int\!\sqrt{(x')^2+(y')^2}\,dt\).

Problem 2 — Polar: total area of the cardioid \(r=1+\sin\theta\)

Given \(r(\theta)=1+\sin\theta\), \(0\le\theta\le2\pi\).

Find the total area enclosed by the curve.

  1. Polar area. \(\displaystyle A=\frac12\int_{0}^{2\pi}(1+\sin\theta)^2\,d\theta\).
  2. Evaluate. \(\displaystyle A=\boxed{\tfrac{3\pi}{2}}\approx 4.7124\).
Problem 3 — Conic in polar: identify type, eccentricity, and directrix

Given \( \displaystyle r=\frac{10}{3-2\cos\theta} \).

Find the eccentricity \(e\), the directrix, and the conic type.

  1. Match to standard. \(\displaystyle r=\frac{ed}{1-e\cos\theta}=\frac{\tfrac{10}{3}}{1-\tfrac{2}{3}\cos\theta}\Rightarrow e=\tfrac23,\ d=5.\)
  2. Classify. \(e=\tfrac23<1\Rightarrow\) ellipse; since it’s \(1-e\cos\theta\), the directrix is \(x=+d=\boxed{5}\).
